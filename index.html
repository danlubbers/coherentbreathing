Coherent breathing, also known as resonant breathing, is a type of breathing that involves taking five to six breaths per minute, which results in 5.5 second inhales and exhales. Each breath should feel like a circle.

Coherent breathing can help to calm the body by affecting the autonomic nervous system (ANS). It can also affect the phrenic nerve, which improves blood circulation, and the amygdala, a part of the brain that regulates emotions, fear, and anxiety.
